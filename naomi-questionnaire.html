01 Core
Core identity & intent
The minimum viable truth. These questions establish who this person is, why they're here, and what they're genuinely looking for. Asked during the Naomi voice call — conversationally, not as a form.
12 questions All required ~3 min Voice-first
L1–01
RequiredIntent
What brings you here today?
🎤 Voice / text · Open-ended
Why this mattersReveals emotional state and readiness. The difference between "I'm tired of apps" and "I want to build a family" is everything. Sets the whole tone.
L1–02
RequiredIntent
What kind of relationship are you looking for?
🎤 Voice · Single-select (Serious / Marriage / Open relationship / Not sure yet)
Why this mattersHard filter. Incompatible relationship goals are the #1 reason matches fail. Must be captured before any matching occurs.
L1–03
RequiredIntent
Tell me about the last person you loved. What made it work — and what didn't?
🎤 Voice · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the workshop Miro board: "Tell me about the last person you loved." Surfaces attachment patterns, self-awareness, and what they've actually learned — not what they claim to want.
L1–04
RequiredLifestyle
What does a typical week look like for you? Walk me through it.
🎤 Voice · Open-ended
Why this mattersSchedule compatibility is underrated. A night-owl entrepreneur and an early-morning teacher can deeply love each other and still make each other miserable. Reveals priorities, not just habits.
L1–05
RequiredIntent
Do you have children? And how do you feel about having them in the future?
🎤 Voice · Structured (Yes/No + future intent)
Why this mattersA dealbreaker that's too uncomfortable to raise on a first date. Naomi surfaces it first so neither person wastes months discovering incompatibility.
L1–06
RequiredLifestyle
Where are you living now, and how rooted are you there?
🎤 Voice · Location + relocation openness
Why this mattersGeographic compatibility matters more than proximity. Someone in Manhattan who'd happily move is very different from someone who'd never leave their neighborhood.
L1–07
RequiredPersonality
How would your closest friends describe you — and would you agree?
🎤 Voice · Open-ended
Why this mattersThe gap between how someone is seen and how they see themselves is one of the most reliable signals of self-awareness. A person who agrees, disagrees, or laughs — all reveal something different.
L1–08
RequiredPersonality
What do you do for work — and how much of you does it take up?
🎤 Voice · Open-ended
Why this mattersNot a career-matching question. A gauge of identity-work fusion. Some people are their job; others have a job. Partners need to be compatible on this, not just in profession.
L1–09
RequiredValues
What are you non-negotiable about in a partner? The things that, if missing, you'd walk away.
🎤 Voice · Open-ended dealbreakers
Why this mattersExplicit dealbreakers are the fastest filters we have. People know these viscerally even when they can't articulate what they want. More reliable than positive wish-lists.
L1–10
RequiredValues
What values are most important to you in a relationship?
🎤 Voice · Open-ended or multi-select
Why this mattersFoundation of the Relationship DNA. Values alignment predicts long-term compatibility better than personality similarity. This seeds the core matching vector.
L1–11
RequiredPersonality
One word — just one — that describes you right now. Don't overthink it.
🎤 Voice / text · Single word
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Forces a moment of genuine self-reflection. The word people choose (or struggle to choose) tells you more than a paragraph. Shows present-state emotional awareness.
L1–12
RequiredIntent
What would a successful match with Naomi look like for you, one year from now?
🎤 Voice · Open-ended vision
Why this mattersGrounds their expectation in something concrete. Also identifies unrealistic expectations early. The person who says "in a serious relationship" vs. "married with a plan" — both valid, both very different.
02 Character
Character & values
Moves past intent into who they actually are. These questions are designed to feel like conversation, not interrogation. Asked during the post-call questionnaire phase, before DNA is revealed.
14 questions 10 required · 4 optional ~3–4 min Text with voice option
L2–01
RequiredPersonality
If you could wake up tomorrow having gained one quality or ability, what would it be?
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank (36 Questions). Reveals the gap between who they are and who they aspire to be. Surfaces insecurities, ambitions, and what they believe they're currently missing — all useful matching signals.
L2–02
RequiredValues
What do you value most in a friendship? (And does the same apply to romantic partnerships?)
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. The overlap between friendship values and relationship values reveals how they think about intimacy and closeness — and whether they seek a partner who is also a best friend.
L2–03
RequiredLifestyle
Would you rather vacation in a big city or the countryside? What does that say about you?
✏️ Text · Structured + reflection
Why this mattersA deceptively simple lifestyle signal with real matching utility. The follow-up ("what does that say about you?") pushes past preference into self-awareness — which is the more valuable data point.
L2–04
RequiredPersonality
What's something you irrationally hate? (Be honest.)
✏️ Text · Open-ended, humor-forward
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Irrational preferences and pet peeves reveal personality more honestly than positive self-descriptions. Also signals sense of humor and willingness to be genuine. Compatibility on the small things matters in daily life.
L2–05
RequiredPersonality
What are you currently obsessed about?
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Current obsessions reveal intellectual energy, passion level, and what someone is actually alive about right now — not just their job title or hobbies list. Shared curiosity is a powerful connector.
L2–06
RequiredValues
What's something you wish you were better at saying "no" to?
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Surfaces boundary-setting habits, people-pleasing tendencies, and self-awareness around personal limits. Critical for predicting how someone navigates conflict and prioritizes their own needs in a relationship.
L2–07
RequiredValues
How do you define success — personally, not professionally?
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. The professional definition is a shield. The personal one reveals what they're actually optimizing for in life — which is what their partner will have to align with. Ambition compatibility matters here.
L2–08
RequiredLifestyle
What does your ideal Sunday look like? Not the fantasy version — the real one.
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersInspired by "what would a perfect day look like?" from the KYC bank, but grounded in reality. A Sunday is the most honest day — no obligations to hide behind. Social/solo, structured/spontaneous, energy level: all revealed here.
L2–09
RequiredPersonality
What music gives you actual goosebumps?
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Music taste is a proxy for emotional openness, aesthetic sensibility, and the kind of beauty someone lets in. "Goosebumps" — not just "favorite" — forces a more honest, less curated answer.
L2–10
RequiredLifestyle
What's something you made recently — anything counts, from a meal to a decision to a piece of work.
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Reveals agency, creativity, and what someone considers worth making. The breadth of "anything counts" is deliberate — it equalizes across different life contexts and surfaces what someone is actively engaged in.
L2–11
OptionalLifestyle
Do you have any superstitions? And do you take them seriously?
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. An unexpected window into how someone relates to uncertainty and control. Also a tone signal — the way they answer reveals humor, self-awareness, and openness to playfulness.
L2–12
OptionalPersonality
If you're sitting next to someone on a plane and they fall asleep but you need the bathroom — do you wake them or shimmy past?
✏️ Text · Behavioral micro-choice
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank (opening question). Reveals conflict-avoidance vs. directness in a completely non-threatening context. How someone handles small social discomfort predicts larger relational patterns.
L2–13
OptionalLifestyle
What's on your bucket list that nobody would ever guess?
✏️ Text · Open-ended surprise
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. The curveball bucket-list item reveals the part of someone they keep private or feel slightly embarrassed about wanting. That's the most interesting part of a person — and often where genuine connection starts.
L2–14
OptionalPersonality
What fashion trend did you follow that seemed perfect then, but now looks a little ridiculous?
✏️ Text · Light / humorous
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Signals willingness to be self-deprecating and non-defensive. People who can laugh at their past selves tend to be easier to be in a relationship with. Also just a great conversation opener.
03 Depth
Depth & reflection
The questions that separate shallow profiles from genuine understanding. Mostly optional — offered after the Relationship DNA reveal, when the user trusts Naomi enough to go deeper. Each answer meaningfully improves match precision.
16 questions 4 required · 12 optional ~3–5 min Text-first (reflective tone)
L3–01
RequiredDepth
What has genuinely surprised you recently — about yourself, someone else, or the world?
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Openness to surprise is one of the strongest signals of intellectual humility and curiosity. Someone who can still be surprised is still growing. This also reveals what they pay attention to — a proxy for worldview.
L3–02
RequiredDepth
Is there something you've dreamed of doing for a long time but haven't done yet? What's holding you back?
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank (Arthur Aron's 36 Questions). The second part — "what's holding you back?" — is where the real signal lives. It separates circumstances from psychology. Fear, practicality, procrastination: each reveals a different person.
L3–03
RequiredDepth
What's the hardest thing you've ever done — and what did it teach you?
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Resilience and self-knowledge in one question. The "what did it teach you?" part is critical — someone who learned vs. someone who endured vs. someone who is still in it are all very different partners.
L3–04
RequiredValues
If you could change anything about the way you were raised, what would it be?
✏️ Text · Deep / sensitive
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank (Arthur Aron's 36 Questions). One of the most revealing questions in existence. What someone answers here — or refuses to answer — tells you about family dynamics, unresolved wounds, and what they're unconsciously replicating or avoiding in relationships.
L3–05
OptionalDepth
When did you last cry in front of another person? What made it happen?
✏️ Text · Vulnerable
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank (Arthur Aron's). Emotional vulnerability and the ability to express it in front of others is one of the strongest predictors of intimacy capacity. Both parts matter: frequency of crying, and willingness to do it with someone present.
L3–06
OptionalDepth
If a crystal ball could tell you one truth about yourself, your life, or the future — what would you ask?
✏️ Text · Reflective
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. What someone most wants to know reveals what they're most uncertain or anxious about. It's a gentle path to the fears and questions they carry around without being able to say them directly.
L3–07
OptionalPersonality
Before making a phone call, do you ever rehearse what you're going to say? Why — or why not?
✏️ Text · Behavioral insight
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. This small behavior maps onto a much larger pattern — preparation vs. spontaneity, anxiety vs. ease, need for control vs. comfort with uncertainty. It's an indirect but precise measure of social anxiety and communication style.
L3–08
OptionalDepth
What's your most treasured memory? You don't have to explain why — just share it.
✏️ Text · Emotional / open
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. What someone chooses as most treasured reveals what they consider the peak of their emotional life. The invitation to skip the "why" reduces performance — you get the real memory, not the one that sounds good.
L3–09
OptionalPersonality
What would you do if you had no fear? Not zero obligations — just no fear.
✏️ Text · Imaginative / revealing
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Distinguishes practical constraints from emotional limits. The answer reveals desires that fear is suppressing — which is different from what circumstances are preventing. Tells you about dreams they haven't said aloud.
L3–10
OptionalLifestyle
What's one goal you're genuinely trying to accomplish this year?
✏️ Text · Present-focused
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. A current goal is a window into someone's active priorities — what they're moving toward right now. Compatibility here is time-sensitive: two people with wildly different present-focus struggle to sync rhythms even when long-term goals align.
L3–11
OptionalPersonality
What's your proudest accomplishment — the one that still makes you feel something?
✏️ Text · Open-ended
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. The emotional qualifier ("still makes you feel something") prevents the polished LinkedIn answer. Reveals what the person genuinely values about themselves versus what they've been told to be proud of.
L3–12
OptionalDepth
Which historical period would you most want to visit — and what does that say about you?
✏️ Text · Reflective + fun
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Historical periods represent value clusters — romantics choose renaissance Italy, pragmatists choose specific decades for what was being built, contrarians choose the uncomfortable ones. The "what does that say?" surfaces self-awareness without requiring introspection explicitly.
L3–13
OptionalLifestyle
Tell me about an adventure you went on recently — could be big, could be a Tuesday.
✏️ Text · Narrative
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. "Could be a Tuesday" gives permission for small adventures, which reveals whether someone finds life interesting at a baseline level or only during major events. Adventure mentality shows in the ordinary.
L3–14
OptionalDepth
Have you ever had an experience that made you genuinely wonder if there's more to the world than we can explain?
✏️ Text · Spiritual / curious
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Spiritual and metaphysical openness is a significant compatibility dimension that most apps ignore. Long-term partners need some alignment here — not identical beliefs, but compatible openness to mystery.
L3–15
OptionalPersonality
What personality trait would you change about yourself if you could — and have you tried?
✏️ Text · Growth-oriented
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. The "have you tried?" distinguishes wishful self-awareness from active growth. Someone who knows what they want to change and is working on it is a very different partner than someone who's said it to themselves for a decade without moving.
L3–16
OptionalPersonality
What's a film you'd make everyone watch? Not your favorite — the one you think says something true about being human.
✏️ Text · Aesthetic / values signal
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. The distinction between "favorite" and "says something true" is key. It moves from entertainment preference to worldview. The film someone chooses as universally necessary reveals their conception of what matters about being alive.
04 DNA
Relationship DNA
The final layer — asked after the first match notification, or progressively unlocked as users engage. These questions complete the compatibility model and are never shown as a block. Naomi surfaces them one at a time, when relevant.
10 questions All optional · Contextual Progressive over weeks In-app, text only
L4–01
OptionalDepth
What does a happy family look like to you — specifically, not in the abstract?
✏️ Text · Vision / values
Why this mattersSurfaces the actual family template someone carries — usually inherited from their own family, consciously or not. The "specifically" qualifier is critical — it gets past the aspirational answer into the behavioral pattern they're actually modeled on.
L4–02
OptionalValues
How do you think finances should work in a serious relationship? Merged, split, flexible — and why?
✏️ Text · Practical values
Why this mattersMoney is the #1 source of relationship conflict. Not because of amounts, but because of different philosophies. This question reveals independence vs. merging orientation, control dynamics, and how someone thinks about "us" vs. "me" at a practical level.
L4–03
OptionalDepth
What would you most like to do for someone else if you had unlimited time and money?
✏️ Text · Generosity / values
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. When someone can do anything for anyone, the choice they make reveals their core generosity orientation. Do they think of family, strangers, causes, or their partner? Each answer maps onto a different relational archetype.
L4–04
OptionalValues
What obligation, if any, do you feel toward your community or country — and does that show up in your daily life?
✏️ Text · Civic / values
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Civic and social responsibility orientation is a significant values dimension for long-term compatibility. The "does it show up?" clause tests for alignment between stated values and actual behavior — a critical signal for authenticity.
L4–05
OptionalDepth
When did you last sing to yourself? Where were you?
✏️ Text · Light / emotional
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank (Arthur Aron's 36 Questions). Seemingly trivial, actually profound. Singing to yourself is a joy behavior — unself-conscious, private, present. It appears when someone is comfortable in their own skin. The "where" tells you what contexts feel safe and alive for them.
L4–06
OptionalDepth
Would you rather meet your great-grandparents or your great-great-grandchild? Why?
✏️ Text · Time-orientation
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. A fascinating proxy for time orientation — past vs. future, roots vs. legacy. Someone deeply connected to their heritage chooses backward; someone driven by what they're creating chooses forward. Both are valid, both predict different relationship rhythms.
L4–07
OptionalPersonality
If you lived to be 100, what would you rather have — a sharp mind or a healthy body? What does that reveal about how you think about aging?
✏️ Text · Philosophical
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. A proxy for what someone considers most essential to self. Mind-first people often approach relationships differently from body-first people — different ideas of dignity, presence, and what it means to still be "here." Deeply relevant for long-term partnership compatibility.
L4–08
OptionalValues
What makes a house a home for you?
✏️ Text · Lifestyle / values
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. Domestic philosophy in a small question. The home environment a person creates reflects their deepest comfort needs — and whoever they share it with will have to live there too. Reveals warmth orientation, nesting instincts, and what "belonging" means to them physically.
L4–09
OptionalDepth
Who is the most inspirational person alive today, in your view — and why them?
✏️ Text · Admiration / values projection
Why this mattersFrom the KYC bank. We project our own highest values onto who we most admire. The "why them?" reveals what the person considers most worth aspiring to — which is what they'll also expect from a long-term partner, consciously or not.
L4–10
OptionalDepth
What's one thing about how you show up in relationships that you're still figuring out?
✏️ Text · Self-awareness / growth
Why this mattersNAOMI-original. The final question of the system. Not "what's your weakness" — but what you're still actively figuring out. The degree of honesty, specificity, and self-compassion in the answer tells you more about relationship readiness than almost anything else asked.
Design principles

Every question earns its place. If Naomi can't explain why an answer improves a match, the question doesn't ship.

Never a form
Questions surface in conversation. The AI connects the dots invisibly.
Always optional at L3–L4
Skipping a deep question is itself a signal. The system learns either way.
No right answers
Questions are scored for compatibility between people, not against an ideal profile.
Progressive trust
Depth of questions increases only as Naomi has earned the right to ask.
